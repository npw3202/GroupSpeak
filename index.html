Voice To Text App

With our Voice to Text app, we want deaf people to be involved in conservations so they won't be left out and let their ideas be heard.

  • Register Phones

For any hearing person, hearing others in a conservation is easy. This task is difficult for deaf people. Some rely on access services like interpreting but a interpreter can't follow a deaf person all day. Some rely on lip reading, but it is not always accurate. Other ways are writing down what the group is saying or texting to each other. However, since oral communcation is the preferred method of almost all hearing individuals, paper or using the phone is not the most efficient way to get the message across.

How to use this app:

-Please have all phones go to this website.

-Register the phones.

-Tell all phones the deaf user id number.

-Leave the phones near their respective owners.

-As the conservation begins, the deaf user will read the conservation on their phone.

-When the conservation is over, the deaf user need to click, "Turn off conservation" on their phones.

-After the conservation is turned off, the server will telleach phone user what words they used more often than others. Then the server will show them the American Sign Language signs for those words.
